GREEK SALAD

I love a good green salad ~ mixed greens, spinach, and kale are some of my favorite starting points for fresh, healthy meals. But summer is a time when all vegetables are at their peak. Sweet, juicy tomatoes, snappy bell peppers, and crisp cucumbers abound. This Greek salad recipe showcases these veggies, without any greens to get in the way.

INGREDIENTS

DRESSING

  • ¼ cup of extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons of red wine vinegar
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • ½ teaspoon of dried oregano, more for sprinkling
  • ¼ teaspoon of sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

FOR THE SALAD

  • 1 English cucumber, cut lengthwise, seeded, and sliced ¼-inch thick
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 cups of halved cherry tomatoes
  • 5 ounces of feta cheese, cut into ½-inch cubes
  • 1/3 cup of thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/3 cup of pitted kalamata olives
  • 1/3 cup of fresh mint leaves

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Make the dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, vinegar, garlic, oregano, mustard, salt, and several grinds of pepper.
  2. On a large platter, arrange the cucumber, green pepper, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, red onions, and olives. Drizzle with the dressing and very gently toss. Sprinkle with a few generous pinches of oregano and top with the mint leaves. Season to taste and serve.
